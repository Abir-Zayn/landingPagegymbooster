How important is manganese for physical health

Manganese is an essential trace element that plays a significant role in numerous physiological processes in the body. It supports antioxidant functions by serving as a cofactor for the enzyme superoxide dismutase and protects cells from oxidative stress. Furthermore, manganese contributes significantly to the formation and maintenance of bones and cartilage, making it essential for bone health. It is also crucial for energy metabolism and lipid metabolism.

Common reasons for manganese deficiency

Although rare, a manganese deficiency can still have serious consequences. Symptoms include weakness, fatigue, joint and bone pain, and impaired wound healing. People with specific dietary habits, such as those who consume little fresh fruit and vegetables, or those with chronic diseases that can impair nutrient absorption, are particularly at risk.

In addition, inadequate intake often occurs in people with chronic diseases that impair the absorption of nutrients from food. These include intestinal diseases such as Crohn's disease or celiac disease, in which nutrient absorption in the digestive tract is impaired. High calcium intake or elevated iron concentrations, the body can also impair manganese absorption, as these minerals compete with each other and influence each other's absorption.

Manganese preparations as support for deficiency symptoms

A manganese deficiency can be effectively compensated for with targeted dietary supplements. Suitable supplements provide the trace element in a concentrated form, ensuring adequate intake even under special circumstances. Manganese supplements work by directly supplying the body with the necessary trace element. This can alleviate symptoms such as fatigue, bone pain, or impaired wound healing, and promote general health. Although various forms of manganese are used in dietary supplements (e.g., manganese bisglycinate, manganese gluconate, or manganese citrate), there is still no clear information on the relative bioavailability of the compounds. Therefore, particular attention should be paid to the individual's tolerance of the supplements.
